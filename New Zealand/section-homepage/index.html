About New Zealand




New Zealand is an island country and a British colony that is comprised of two islands, the north and the south, along with other smaller islands off the coast. New Zealand is a remote country, it is located 1,600km south-east from it's closest neighbour Australia. New Zealand's capital is Wellington, however the most populated city is Auckland at 1,606,564 people. New Zealand also has a very diverse climate, it has everything from active volcanoes to glaciers and many divers species of flora and fauna

The People of New Zealand



There are many types of people in New Zealand, people of many different ethnicities and backgrounds.


New Zealand was one of the last large landmasses to be widely populated. The orignial settlers were of Polynesian decent and are commonly known as Maori today. Europeans arrived later on (mainly Scottish), but not before they had introduced new diseases quickly decreasing the Mauri population to around 100,000.







Fun Facts



  • New Zealand's national rugby team, the All-Blacks, is one of the best teams in the world and have held the number-one position in the world more than all the other teams combined.

  • The famous movie trilogy 'The Lord of the Rings', was filmed in it's entirety in New Zealand. It was filmed on both the north and south island over the course of 438 days.

  • New Zealand is the birthplace of the first person to ever reach the summit of Mt. Everest, Sir Edmund Hillary. Hillary was born in Auckland and lived there for most of his life. He is even on the $5 note in New Zealand.

  • There are more sheep than people in New Zealand, with a ratio of nine sheep to every one human - approximately 40 million sheep total.

  • Acaemy award winning director Taika Waititi is of New Zealand decent. He has directed films like; award winning movie 'Jojo Rabbit'; 'Thor: Ragnarok'; 'Hunt for the Wilderpeople' and 'What We Do in the Shadows'.
